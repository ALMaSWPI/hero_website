Our Motivation!

In the past 20 years, there have been over 7 thousand natural disasters, losing 1.23 million lives and affecting 4.2 billion people. Often times these disasters create hostile environments where human search-and-rescue missions are too dangerous. Using humanoid robots for search and rescue eliminates the danger of sending search parties in to high-risk environments, greatly impacting disaster relief efforts. All in all, humanoid robots can be used in place of human rescuers for a much safer emergency response in these situations.

Our Goals!

This project aims to manufacture, design, and control the lower half of a self-balancing bipedal robot, named HURON. HURON will be able to react to forces anywhere on the body and move accordingly to regain balance, and exhibit a human-like gait for walking. Undergraduate and graduate students will work on this project for the next several years and innovate the humanoid to survive in hazardous environments such as the aftermaths for fires, floods, and sand or duststorms.

HURON will be an excellent educational research platform for future undergraduate and graduate students. Currently, there are no bipedal robots used for these tragedies and with our cost-effective design, HURON could potentially be utilized to aid natural disasters all over the world.

Self-Balancing

HURON will be able to balance itself as well as stabilize itself in case of external forces being applied

Human-like Gait

Gait refers to a person's pattern of walking. HURON will be able to walk with human-like gait.

Designs match human anatomy

Not only can HURON walk with human-like gait, but also it has been designed in proportions that match human anatomy
